## Bank — Marlowe & Finch (lines 44–71)

### Open the first envelope (L51)
On the desk, an envelope with no return address. Inside, a single hand-painted card — **The Wheel of Fortune**, the numeral **X** in the corner — and a note in looping script.

> No fortune favors the unannounced. What is locked will open.

No date. No demand. Only the promise. The card is yours now; it joins *The Cards*.

### Unscrew the brass air vent (L57)
The screws turn too easily — their slots are bright where a fresh tool bit into the brass. Someone has had this grate off recently, and put it back with care. The shaft beyond runs down toward the vault corridor, just wide enough for a thin man.

### Pull the maintenance ledger (L61)
Years of signatures for work on the locks and the vault door. One name recurs through the early entries, the contractor who overhauled the mechanism six years ago: **FELIX MARSH, locksmith**.

### Open the visitors' book (L66)
Most entries are dull — couriers, clerks, the auditor. One oddity: the tenor **Enrico Belloni** signed in three times in a single week this spring, each visit only minutes long. A famous voice has no business in a bank vault three times in seven days.

## Madame Vesna's Shop (lines 72–121)

### Dialogue (L76–108)

**Show her a painted card**
She turns the Wheel over in her hands and her mouth tightens. *"The card design looks so similar to my own deck! Someone must have copied them - someone with artisanal talents. However, a trained eye will be able to spot the differences between this and my original deck."*

**Borrow her original deck**
She wraps the deck in black silk and presses it into your hands. *"Mine are honest. Lay them beside the forgeries on your own table. Where they differ, the copyist meant something."* Vesna's true cards now sit on the table beside yours.

**Ask about Felix Marsh**
*"Felix. Yes — a client from years ago, and an avid tarot reader himself, better than most."* She almost smiles. *"But he never read under his own name. In the circle, he goes by the alias **The Fool** — the wanderer who carries nothing, fears nothing. He has a peculiar ways of reading his cards...* Vesna furrows her brows. *"Felix cut the deck upside down. Every card he read **reversed** — always. 'Fortune only tells me the truth standing on its head,' he used to say. It drove the others mad. He never once read a card the right way up."*

### Hotspots (L109–120)

**Study the lunar almanac on the shelf**
An almanac, open to **June**. The month's phases are marked clearly on the almanac.

**Glance at the crystal ball**
You lean toward the glass, and see your own face, upside down and small. Hmm, I wonder if this crystal ball will tell me anything useful...

## Scotland Yard (lines 122–139)

### Pull the bank's insurance schedule (L134)
The schedule shows Marlowe & Finch's safes that are covered by insurance. The top five most valuable safes are:
- Box 12 - Enrico Belloni - Assorted heirlooms. Original music scrolls by Andrea Bocelli, of whom Enrico was the protégé.
- Box 35 - Dr. Nicholas Neo - Contents declared in a single word: "priceless".
- Box 52 - Taylor Swift - Longhand love letters from ex-boyfriends.
- Box 78 - Elon Musk - Bearer bonds, share certificates. *(and perhaps some dirty secrets too?)*
- Box 96 - Queen Victoria - Jewellery, fully appraised.

### Read Felix Marsh's bio (L128)
No past criminal records. Felix Marsh, locksmith, lost his savings last year when a fund manager defrauded his clients. The fund's reclusive backer, the multimillionaire **Dr. Nicholas Neo**, repaid every investor from his own pocket. Felix **refused the money** — too proud to take charity from a rich man.

### Read Enrico Belloni's bio
Petty crimes during his teenage years. Used to be involved with the Italian mafia. Now, he's a well-respected, well-paid member of the Opera, and a famous philanthropist and patron of the arts. A true inspirations to kids who grew up rough. *Is this heroic font just a facade?*

## Felix's Workshop (lines 140–166)

### Examine the practice vault door (L146)
A full vault door, bolted to the workshop wall and drilled a hundred times over — the same door the bank uses, or near enough. The floor beneath is bright with metal filings. He has done this entry, in the dark of his head, more times than you can count.

### Unpin the card above the door (L150)
Pinned dead-centre above the practice door, where a man would see it every working hour: **The Fool**, numbered **0**. It joins *The Cards*.

### Photograph the wall calendar (L155)
A cheap wall calendar, June showing. One date is ringed in pencil, gone over and over until the lead tore the paper: **the 11th**.

### Search the wall of orphaned keys (L161)
Dozens of keys on numbered hooks, every tag aged to the same brown — except one, newer than the rest, the ink barely faded: **"The Crown & Anchor, cellar — NOT FINISHED."**

## Royal Opera House (lines 167–189)

### Search Enrico's dressing room (L184)
You look around the extravagant gilded dressing room that must have been called the second home by many Opera legends. A single card jumps out to you amongst all the items on the vanity set: the **Seven of Swords** — a thief carrying off five blades and leaving two standing behind him. It joins *The Cards*.

### Ask the stage doorkeeper (L173)
The old doorkeeper knows Belloni's habits to the minute. *"Before every opening night he sends a boy to fetch a little parcel from the bank, and the morning after he sends it straight back. Never opens it where anyone can see. Superstition, with singers — you learn not to ask."*

### Ask the tenor's dresser (L178)
The dresser lowers her voice and whispers in thick italian accent: *"It's his mother's brooch. She passed when he was just a bambino. He wears it pinned inside his waistcoat, against the heart - some kind of lucky charm. Poor boy misses his mama every single day."*

## The Crown & Anchor (lines 190–207)

### Ask the landlady about Felix (L196)
*"Felix? Course I knew Felix. Rebuilding my cellar door all winter, good work too — then one week in spring last year he just stopped coming."* She nods at the wall. *"Left his tab unpaid too. Not like him. Something took him off and never gave him back."*

### Examine the unfinished cellar door (L201)
The work is immaculate as far as it goes. This Felix Marsh must be quite a master at his work. His tools are still in their canvas roll on the floor, laid out in order, abandoned.
